What Are Cookies?

A simple explanation

No technical jargon — just what you need to know.

Think of cookies as a small "memory" that a website leaves on your computer or phone. This memory helps the website remember things about your visit — like if you've seen a notice before or your preferences — so you don't have to repeat actions every time you come back.

For example, when you accept our privacy notice, a small "note" is saved on your device. The next time you visit, the website checks this note and knows you've already agreed, so it won't show you the same notice again.

What We Don't Do

We believe in being clear about what we don't collect or track — just as much as what we do.

We Don't Track Your Activity

We don't use tools to watch what you click, how long you stay, or what pages you visit. We respect your privacy.

No Ads, No Tracking

We don't show ads, and we don't save any information to show you ads later. No advertiser can track you through our site.

We Don't Profile You

We don't build a picture of who you are based on what you do on this site. We don't know who you are unless you tell us.

No Outside Trackers

We don't use cookies or trackers from other companies. If you click a link to another site, their own privacy rules apply.

Your Choice Stays on Your Device

The note about your privacy preference is saved only on your own device. It is never sent to us or stored on our servers.

We Don't Follow You Elsewhere

We don't track your activity on other websites before or after you visit iProcessph.com. Your browsing stays your own.

— 04

Managing Your Cookies

You are always in control. Here's how to manage what we save on your device and how to clear it.

Reset Your Privacy Choice

If you want to see the privacy notice again (for example, to change your choice), you can clear just that one saved note. If you're comfortable with a simple step, you can clear all saved data for this site through your browser settings.

How: In most browsers, go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Clear browsing data → select "Cookies and other site data".

Browser Controls

All major browsers let you block or limit what websites can save on your device. If you choose to block everything, you'll see the privacy notice every time you visit since we can't remember your choice.

Guides for: Chrome · Firefox · Safari · Edge

Updates to This Policy

If we ever change how we use cookies or start saving new information, we will update this page and let you know. We are committed to keeping this information clear and current.
